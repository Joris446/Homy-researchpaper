Abstract

People with dementia often face challenges in navigating outdoor spaces and therefore do not always go outside anymore. This study explores Homy, a voice-based wayfinding device designed to support both wayfinding and meaningful engagement with outdoor surroundings. A field study with four participants and two caregivers showed the potential of Homy to effectively guide users through voice prompts and meanwhile redirected attention to nature and nearby objects, triggering reflection and emotional responses. This paper provides lessons learned and best practices for designing wayfinding devices for people with dementia.
